The Factual Wing: The Four Pillars

I & II: The Cognitive Revolution & Externalized Thought

The first two pillars are intertwined. The emergence of abstract language allowed for the creation of shared fictions—myths, gods, and tribal identities—that acted as a "social glue" for large-scale cooperation. This was immediately followed by the invention of external memory: art and symbols. By inscribing their thoughts onto cave walls and bone, humans created a persistent, shared database.

III: The Agricultural Revolution

The shift to long-term planning created the first significant food surpluses in history. This triggered a phase transition in social complexity, leading to population density and the specialization of labor. This was the birth of the city.

IV: The Invention of Law

Dense societies created a new crisis: social friction. The final "unlock" was impersonal, codified law. A written legal code created a single, objective "operating system" for civilization, allowing thousands of strangers to cooperate, trust, and build within a shared framework.
